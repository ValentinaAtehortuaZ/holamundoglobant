Harry Potter and the Sorcerer's Stone

Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone (Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone) is the first book in the series, it was published in the United Kingdom on June 26, 1997 and in Spanish in March 1999.It is one of the best-selling books of history, estimates of its worldwide sales exceed 110 million copies. In the spring of 2007, a first edition signed by Rowling was auctioned in London for £27,876. In this first work, most of the main characters of the series are introduced, as well as many of the places where the action will take place. Harry's first steps in the world of magic are narrated, as well as his first confrontation with Voldemort, who in his search for immortality wants to obtain the power of the philosopher's stone.

Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets

Harry Potter and the secret chamber (Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets) was originally published on July 2, 1998, and in Spanish in October 1999. The book chronicles Harry's second year at Hogwarts. One day an elf named Dobby came to Harry's house to warn him that Hogwarts was in grave danger. Later his friend Ron will pick him up in a flying car and thus begins his course at Hogwarts during which messages appear on the walls of the school corridors warning that the Chamber of Secrets has been opened, followed by a series of attacks students who do not come from families with magical blood. In this installment they introduce the figure of the house elf and characters relevant to the rest of the series, such as Lucius Malfoy, Ginny Weasley and Arthur Weasley, as well as revealing a little more about Voldemort's past through his personal diary.

Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban

Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban (Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban) was published in English on July 8, 1999, while in Spanish it was published in April 2000. This was the fastest book Rowling wrote, since it finished in just a year after starting to write it. It was also the recipient of the Costa Award and the Bram Stoker Award, among others, which place it as one of the most successful fantastic books of recent years. This time the figure of the dementor and the characters of Remus Lupin and Sirius Black are introduced, who at the beginning of the novel escapes from Azkaban prison, in addition to developing the story of Harry's parents. It is the only book in the series in which Voldemort does not appear.

Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire

Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire (Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire) was published in English on July 8, 2000 and in Spanish in March 2001. The size of the book increased considerably compared to the first three, an idea of ​​the that Rowling was aware of from the conception of the novel. The title went through various modifications, including Harry Potter and the Doomspeell Tournament, Harry Potter and the Triwizard Tournament, until the author leaned towards The Goblet of Fire because it reminded the concept of the "cup of destiny" , which according to her was the subject of the book.14 The novel was the winner of the Hugo Award for best novel in 2001. On this occasion, Harry's fourth year at Hogwarts is narrated and the mystery surrounding the involuntary entry of his name into the Triwizard Tournament, in which he is forced to compete along with three other participants. The story further explores the wizarding world and ends with the resurgence of Lord Voldemort. Prior to the book's publication, much controversy and anticipation was generated by the author's announcement that a character would die.

Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix

Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix (Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix) is with almost 900 pages in its English edition16 the longest book in the series, a fact that the author herself considers a defect. It was published worldwide in English on June 21, 2003, and in Spanish on February 21, 2004.The Spanish edition by Salamandra consisted of three versions: one for Spain, another for the southern cone and another for Colombia, Mexico and the United States. In the fifth book, Harry Potter must confront both a resurrected Voldemort and the rest of the wizarding world who refuse to believe this to be true, starting with the Ministry of Magic. This names Dolores Umbridge as the new headmistress of Hogwarts, and along with Luna Lovegood and Bellatrix Lestrange they are the three most prominent characters introduced in this installment. On the other hand, an important prophecy is revealed concerning Harry and Voldemort.

Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince

Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince (Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince) was published in English on July 16, 2005 and was presented by Rowling at a press conference reserved only for children between 8 and 16 years old. Almost a year before her original publication, Rowling had expressed her willingness to kill another character on her official website, so a series of unofficial bets took place in which the possibilities of her. In this sixth installment, Harry stumbles across an ancient potions textbook filled with annotations and recommendations signed by a mysterious prince. At the same time, he receives private lessons from the school principal, Albus Dumbledore, who makes him know moments from Voldemort's past, in order to teach him what horcruxes are, elemental objects to achieve his victory. At the end of the book, Professor Severus Snape, whose loyalty was in doubt throughout the series, assassinates Dumbledore. The phrase Snape kills Dumbledore (Snape kills Dumbledore) became an internet phenomenon that prompted all kinds of videos and graphics.

Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows part 1

The seventh novel, Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows (Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows), was published in English on July 21, 2007, closing the decade-long series. The book broke sales records, with over 11 million copies sold in its first 48 hours, in the UK and US alone. The previous mark was held by Half-Blood Prince.25 This last novel narrates the events that directly follow the death of Dumbledore, in which Voldemort completes his rise to power and manages to dominate the Ministry of Magic. Harry and his friends decide not to attend their last year at Hogwarts, to go out in search of the remaining horcruxes. Finally, the battle of Hogwarts takes place, between the Order of the Phoenix, students and teachers of the school, on the one hand, and Voldemort and the Death Eaters, on the other. The novel ends with an epilogue that tells the future of the surviving characters 19 years after the confrontation, showing that each of them has shaped their lives.
